Mulberry polyphenols ameliorate atherogenic migration and proliferation.
2022 Oct 12
Extra proliferation and increased migration of vascular smooth cells contribute to the formation of atherosclerosis. Mulberry (Morus spp., Moraceae), is an economic plant that is widely cultivated. It has been used as a folk medicine for a long time. Extracts from mulberry have pharmacological properties, including antioxidant, anti-inflammation, and anti-diabetes activities Mulberry leaf tea and it major ingredient deoxynojirimycin significantly repressed cerebrovascular and cardiovascular events and reduced carotid intima-media thickness in patients with coronary heart disease in a clinical trial.
